Where does the data come from

This site crawls the hacker news front page every 5 minutes. The front page is the only page that is crawled. If the submission does not make the front page, it will not end up on this site.

Why can't I upvote?

Because that is not possible, due to limitation of Hacher News. If Y Combinator would implement OAuth, it could be done, but I wouldn't hold my breath.

Why the colors?

To make the UI better. The main hacker news front page looks nice, but the over grayness makes it hard to scan the page for information. With the colors, it is easy to tell which stories have the most comments, the most upvotes, and which ones are the newest entries.

What the colors mean

Each data point about a submission is colorized.
Green hues are meant to convey "newness", or "undiscoveredness" (if thats even a word).
Red and orange are meant to convey something that is popular.

Lime Green

  • Points: less than 10
  • Submitter: has a karma score less than 10% of users amoung same registration month
  • Age of Submission: less than 30 minutes
  • Comments: less than 10
